About me

I am a doctoral researcher at TU Delft as part of the SEGUE project. I study the relationship between economic inequality and residential segregation, with particular attention to the role of housing policy. I am also a Law graduate and member of the Madrid Bar Association (n췈 144232). You can find my full CV here.

I work with large-scale administrative microdata, combining spatial analysis with quantitative methods to study income and wealth segregation across different geographic scales. I have also used experimental methods (i.e. correspondence testing) to study housing discrimination against racial minorities.

Alongside my academic work, I have experience in the field of human rights, including preparing and leading submissions on the right to housing before the United Nations Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights. I often collaborate with civil-society organisations, using empirical research to inform public discussions on housing and urban inequality.
